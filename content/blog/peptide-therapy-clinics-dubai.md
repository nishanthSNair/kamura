## What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — typically between 2 and 50 amino acids in length. Think of them as smaller, more targeted versions of proteins. While proteins are large, complex molecules that perform broad functions in the body, peptides act as precise signaling molecules. They tell specific cells what to do: repair tissue, modulate the immune system, stimulate hormone release, or reduce inflammation.

Your body naturally produces thousands of peptides. Insulin, for example, is a peptide hormone. So is oxytocin. Peptide therapy involves introducing synthetic or bioidentical versions of these molecules to trigger specific biological responses — essentially giving your body the instructions it needs to heal, recover, or optimize faster.

### Why Peptides Are Different from Traditional Medications

Unlike broad-spectrum pharmaceuticals that often come with systemic side effects, peptides are highly targeted. They bind to specific receptors and activate precise pathways. This makes them generally well-tolerated with fewer side effects — though they should still be administered under medical supervision.

## The Most Popular Peptides in Longevity Medicine

### BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)

Originally derived from a protective protein found in gastric juice, BPC-157 is one of the most widely used peptides in regenerative medicine. It accelerates wound healing, repairs gut lining, reduces inflammation, and supports tendon and ligament recovery. Athletes and biohackers use it for injury recovery, while longevity practitioners prescribe it for gut health optimization.

**Best for:** Gut healing, injury recovery, tendon and ligament repair, reducing inflammation
**Administration:** Subcutaneous injection near the injury site, or oral capsules for gut applications

### Thymosin Alpha-1

A naturally occurring peptide produced by the thymus gland, Thymosin Alpha-1 is a powerful immune modulator. It enhances the body's ability to fight infections, supports T-cell function, and has been used clinically to treat hepatitis B and C, as well as certain cancers. In the longevity space, it's used to strengthen immune resilience and reduce the frequency and severity of illness.

**Best for:** Immune system support, chronic infection management, immune resilience
**Administration:** Subcutaneous injection, typically 2–3 times per week

### CJC-1295 (with or without DAC)

CJC-1295 is a growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog. It stimulates the pituitary gland to produce more growth hormone naturally, rather than introducing exogenous growth hormone directly. This leads to improved body composition (more muscle, less fat), better sleep quality, enhanced recovery, and improved skin elasticity. It's often paired with Ipamorelin for a synergistic effect.

**Best for:** Growth hormone optimization, body composition, sleep quality, recovery
**Administration:** Subcutaneous injection, typically before bed (to align with natural GH pulsatility)

### Epithalon (Epitalon)

One of the most intriguing peptides in anti-aging research. Epithalon stimulates the production of telomerase, the enzyme that maintains telomere length. Telomeres — the protective caps at the ends of chromosomes — shorten as we age, and their length is considered a biomarker of biological aging. By activating telomerase, Epithalon may slow or partially reverse this aspect of cellular aging.

**Best for:** Anti-aging, telomere maintenance, cellular longevity
**Administration:** Subcutaneous injection or IV infusion, typically administered in 10–20 day cycles

### Other Notable Peptides

- **Ipamorelin** — A selective growth hormone secretagogue with fewer side effects than other GH-releasers. Often stacked with CJC-1295.
- **Selank** — An anxiolytic peptide that reduces anxiety and enhances cognitive function without sedation. Administered as a nasal spray.
- **TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)** — Promotes wound healing, reduces scarring, and supports tissue repair. Popular with athletes.
- **KPV** — An anti-inflammatory peptide showing promise for gut health, particularly in inflammatory bowel conditions.
- **GHK-Cu** — A copper peptide used topically for skin rejuvenation, wound healing, and collagen stimulation.

## How Peptides Are Administered

Peptide therapy delivery methods vary depending on the peptide and the intended effect:

### Subcutaneous Injection

The most common method. A small needle (similar to an insulin syringe) injects the peptide into the fatty tissue just beneath the skin — typically in the abdomen, thigh, or near an injury site. Most patients learn to self-administer at home after initial training at the clinic. The injections are virtually painless.

### Nasal Spray

Some peptides, particularly those targeting cognitive function (like Selank and Semax), are delivered as nasal sprays. This allows rapid absorption through the nasal mucosa and faster delivery to the brain. Convenient and needle-free.

### Oral Capsules

A growing number of peptides are now available in oral form, though bioavailability can be lower due to breakdown in the digestive tract. Oral BPC-157, for example, is specifically effective for gut applications where direct contact with the GI tract is beneficial.

### IV Infusion

Some clinics administer peptides intravenously for maximum bioavailability. This is common for Epithalon and NAD+ (which, while technically a coenzyme, is often discussed alongside peptide protocols). IV delivery ensures 100% absorption but requires clinical administration.

## Regulation and Safety in the UAE

Peptide therapy in the UAE operates within the country's broader medical regulatory framework. Key points to understand:

- **Prescription required:** Peptides are classified as medical treatments and must be prescribed by a licensed physician. You cannot legally purchase injectable peptides over the counter in the UAE.
- **Quality control:** Reputable clinics source pharmaceutical-grade peptides from certified compounding pharmacies. Always ask your clinic about their sourcing.
- **Medical supervision:** The UAE health authorities (DHA in Dubai, DOH in Abu Dhabi) require that injectable treatments be administered or supervised by licensed medical professionals.
- **Growing acceptance:** The UAE has been progressive in embracing regenerative and preventive medicine. Peptide therapy is increasingly mainstream within the medical establishment, particularly at dedicated longevity clinics.

### Red Flags to Watch For

Avoid any provider who:

- Sells peptides without a medical consultation or blood work
- Cannot explain their sourcing or quality control
- Offers peptides without a prescription
- Has no licensed physician overseeing the protocol
- Makes extravagant or guaranteed outcome claims
