## Why the Wellness Community Loves Bone Broth

Bone broth isn't a trend — it's an ancient food that modern science is validating. The key benefits driving its popularity:

### Gut Health
Bone broth is rich in **gelatin** and **glutamine**, two compounds that help repair and maintain the gut lining. For anyone dealing with leaky gut, IBS, or post-antibiotic gut issues, it's often the first thing practitioners recommend.

### Collagen for Skin & Joints
A quality bone broth delivers **Type I, II, and III collagen** — the same types that keep skin firm and joints healthy. Many in the community report improved skin elasticity and reduced joint pain within weeks of daily consumption.

### Fasting Support
Bone broth is a cornerstone of fasting protocols. During Ramadan, many community members use it for suhoor or iftar to:
- Gently break a fast without spiking insulin
- Provide electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium)
- Deliver protein without digestive stress

### Mineral Density
Slow-simmered bones release calcium, magnesium, phosphorus, and trace minerals in a highly bioavailable form — meaning your body actually absorbs them.

## How to Make Your Own

Many in the community prefer homemade broth. Here's the community-approved method:

### Ingredients
- 1–2 kg grass-fed beef bones or organic chicken carcasses
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar (helps extract minerals)
- Filtered water to cover
- Optional: onion, garlic, ginger, turmeric, bay leaves

### Method
1. Place bones in a large pot or slow cooker
2. Add apple cider vinegar and enough water to cover by 2 inches
3. Let sit for 30 minutes (the acid starts extracting minerals)
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer
5. Simmer for **24–48 hours** for beef bones, **12–24 hours** for chicken
6. Strain, cool, and store in glass jars

### The Gel Test
Good bone broth should **gel** when refrigerated. If it stays liquid, it wasn't simmered long enough or didn't have enough collagen-rich bones (knuckles, feet, and joints are best).

### Where to Source Bones
- **Organic butchers** in Al Quoz and Satwa
- **Kibsons** — order organic bones online
- Ask your local butcher for **knuckle bones, oxtail, and marrow bones** — these have the highest collagen content

## What to Look For (Quality Checklist)

When buying bone broth, check for:

- **Grass-fed / organic sourcing** — non-negotiable for avoiding hormones and antibiotics
- **Long simmer time** (18–48 hours) — this is what extracts the good stuff
- **No added fillers** — avoid broths with yeast extract, maltodextrin, or "natural flavors"
- **Gel factor** — quality broth gels when cold
- **Low sodium** — you want to control your own salt levels
- **Protein content** — aim for 10g+ protein per serving (indicates good collagen extraction)
