## What is NAD+?

Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD+) is a coenzyme found in every living cell in your body. It plays a central role in some of the most fundamental biological processes that keep you alive and healthy.

### The key functions of NAD+

- **Cellular energy production** — NAD+ is essential for converting food into ATP, the energy currency your cells run on. Without adequate NAD+, your mitochondria cannot produce energy efficiently, leading to fatigue and reduced cellular function.
- **DNA repair** — Every day, your DNA sustains thousands of breaks and mutations from normal metabolic processes, UV exposure, and environmental toxins. NAD+ activates PARP enzymes, which are critical for detecting and repairing this damage before it accumulates.
- **Sirtuin activation** — Sirtuins are a family of seven proteins (SIRT1-SIRT7) that regulate aging, inflammation, and stress resistance. They are sometimes called "longevity genes," and they require NAD+ to function. When NAD+ levels are high, sirtuins are more active, promoting cellular repair and resilience.
- **Circadian rhythm regulation** — NAD+ levels fluctuate with your circadian clock, and adequate levels support healthy sleep-wake cycles.
- **Immune function** — NAD+ supports the activity of immune cells, particularly CD38+ immune cells involved in inflammatory response regulation.

### The NAD+ decline problem

Here is the challenge: NAD+ levels decline significantly with age. Research published in *Cell Metabolism* has shown that by the time you reach your 40s, your NAD+ levels may be half of what they were in your 20s. By your 60s, they may drop to 25% or less. This decline is associated with many hallmarks of aging — reduced energy, slower recovery, cognitive fog, increased inflammation, and greater susceptibility to age-related diseases.

This is why NAD+ supplementation and IV therapy have generated so much interest in the longevity community. The premise is straightforward: if declining NAD+ contributes to aging, restoring NAD+ levels should slow or partially reverse some of those effects.

## How NAD+ Therapy Works

### IV infusion (the most common clinical method)

The most direct and popular method for boosting NAD+ levels is intravenous (IV) infusion. NAD+ is delivered directly into the bloodstream, bypassing the digestive system for near-complete bioavailability.

**What a typical session looks like:**

1. **Arrival and assessment** — A nurse or clinician will check your vitals, review your health history, and set up a comfortable infusion station. Most clinics offer private or semi-private rooms with recliners.
2. **IV setup** — A small catheter is placed in your arm, and the NAD+ solution (typically 250-500mg per session, sometimes up to 1,000mg for intensive protocols) begins dripping.
3. **The infusion** — This is the long part. NAD+ must be infused slowly because rapid administration can cause uncomfortable side effects including chest tightness, nausea, and a temporary flushing sensation. A standard session takes 2 to 4 hours.
4. **During the drip** — Most people read, work on a laptop, watch content on their phone, or simply rest. Many clinics provide blankets, refreshments, and a calm environment.
5. **Post-infusion** — After the drip completes, you may feel an immediate boost in energy and mental clarity, or the effects may build over the following 24-48 hours. Some people report mild fatigue on the day of infusion before experiencing benefits the next day.

### Other delivery methods

- **Subcutaneous injection** — Faster than IV (15-20 minutes) but lower doses. Some clinics offer this for maintenance between IV sessions.
- **Oral supplements (NMN, NR)** — Nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN) and nicotinamide riboside (NR) are NAD+ precursors taken orally. They are more affordable and convenient but have lower bioavailability than IV infusion. Many longevity practitioners recommend oral precursors for daily maintenance alongside periodic IV sessions.
- **Nasal spray** — Emerging delivery method. Limited clinical data but growing in popularity for convenience.

## Benefits: What the Research Shows

NAD+ therapy research is still evolving, but the existing evidence is promising:

### Strong evidence
- **Cellular energy restoration** — Multiple studies confirm that NAD+ replenishment improves mitochondrial function and ATP production.
- **DNA repair activation** — Research in *Science* (2017) demonstrated that NAD+ supplementation in mice restored DNA repair capacity and reversed age-related tissue decline.
- **Sirtuin activation** — Well-established biochemistry shows NAD+ is required for sirtuin function. Elevated NAD+ levels increase sirtuin activity.

### Emerging evidence
- **Cognitive function** — Animal studies show NAD+ supplementation improves memory and neuroplasticity. Human trials are underway.
- **Anti-aging biomarkers** — A 2023 clinical trial published in *Nature Aging* found that NMN supplementation improved several biomarkers of biological age in middle-aged adults.
- **Cardiovascular health** — Early research suggests NAD+ may improve vascular function and reduce arterial stiffness.

### Anecdotal but widely reported
- Increased sustained energy without the crash of caffeine
- Improved mental clarity and focus
- Better sleep quality
- Faster exercise recovery
- Reduced brain fog, particularly after illness or high-stress periods

## Who Should Consider NAD+ Therapy?

NAD+ therapy may be worth exploring if you:

- Are over 35 and experiencing declining energy, recovery, or mental sharpness
- Have a demanding lifestyle (executive stress, frequent travel, high training loads)
- Are interested in proactive longevity medicine and biological age optimization
- Are recovering from illness or a period of chronic stress
- Want to complement other longevity interventions (exercise, nutrition, sleep optimization)

### Who should proceed with caution

- Pregnant or breastfeeding women (insufficient safety data)
- Those on certain medications (discuss with your prescribing physician)
- People with active cancer (NAD+ may promote cell growth broadly — consult your oncologist)

## How to Maximize Your NAD+ Investment

NAD+ therapy works best as part of a broader health optimization strategy:

1. **Fix your sleep first** — NAD+ levels naturally fluctuate with circadian rhythms. Poor sleep undermines the benefits.
2. **Exercise regularly** — Physical activity naturally stimulates NAD+ production. The combination of exercise and NAD+ supplementation is synergistic.
3. **Consider oral NMN between sessions** — Daily NMN (250-500mg) helps maintain elevated NAD+ levels between IV sessions.
4. **Track your biomarkers** — Request NAD+ level testing before and after your protocol. Clinics like AEON offer this as part of their longevity panels.
5. **Reduce NAD+ depleters** — Excessive alcohol, chronic stress, and poor sleep all accelerate NAD+ decline.
