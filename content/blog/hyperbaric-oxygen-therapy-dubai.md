Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT) is one of the most clinically validated and scientifically compelling treatments in the longevity medicine toolkit. Originally developed for treating decompression sickness in deep-sea divers, HBOT has evolved into a powerful intervention for brain health, wound healing, anti-aging, and performance optimization. Dubai is home to some of the most advanced HBOT facilities in the world, including the largest hyperbaric center globally. Here is everything you need to know before booking your first session.

## How Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy Works

The concept behind HBOT is elegantly simple. You breathe pure oxygen (100% O2) while sitting or lying inside a pressurized chamber. The pressure inside the chamber is increased to 1.5 to 2.0 ATA (atmospheres absolute) — roughly equivalent to the pressure you would experience 5 to 10 meters underwater.

Under these conditions, two things happen:

### 1. Dramatically increased oxygen delivery

At normal atmospheric pressure, the oxygen you breathe is carried almost exclusively by hemoglobin in your red blood cells. Hemoglobin is already about 97% saturated under normal conditions, so simply breathing more oxygen does not significantly increase delivery. However, under hyperbaric pressure, oxygen dissolves directly into your blood plasma, cerebrospinal fluid, and other bodily fluids at concentrations 10 to 15 times higher than normal. This oxygen-rich plasma can reach tissues and areas of the body that red blood cells cannot easily access — including damaged tissue, inflamed areas, and the brain.

### 2. Pressure-driven healing responses

The increased pressure itself triggers beneficial biological responses. It stimulates the release of growth factors, promotes stem cell mobilization from bone marrow, reduces swelling by constricting blood vessels while paradoxically increasing oxygen delivery, and activates genes involved in tissue repair and regeneration.

### The molecular cascade

At the cellular level, HBOT triggers a phenomenon called hyperoxia-hypoxia paradox. When you leave the chamber and return to normal oxygen levels, your body interprets the relative drop as a mild hypoxic signal, which activates HIF-1 (hypoxia-inducible factor) pathways. This stimulates the production of new blood vessels (angiogenesis), enhances mitochondrial function, and upregulates antioxidant defenses. In essence, HBOT trains your cells to use oxygen more efficiently.

## What the Research Shows

### Brain health and cognitive reversal

The most groundbreaking HBOT research comes from a team at Tel Aviv University led by Professor Shai Efrati, whose work is now applied at Aviv Clinics in Dubai. Their landmark 2020 study, published in the journal *Aging*, demonstrated that HBOT could reverse two key hallmarks of biological aging in humans: telomere shortening and senescent cell accumulation. Participants who completed 60 HBOT sessions showed telomere lengthening of up to 20% and a reduction in senescent cells of up to 37%.

A subsequent study published in *Aging* (2021) focused specifically on cognitive function in healthy adults over 64. After 60 HBOT sessions, participants showed significant improvements in attention, information processing speed, and executive function. Brain imaging revealed increased cerebral blood flow and improved microstructural integrity — essentially, the participants' brains were functioning as if they were years younger.

### Wound healing and tissue repair

HBOT is FDA-approved for 14 medical conditions, many related to wound healing. It is a standard treatment for diabetic foot ulcers, radiation injury, compromised skin grafts, and chronic non-healing wounds. The mechanism is clear: oxygen-rich plasma promotes collagen synthesis, fights infection (many harmful bacteria are anaerobic and cannot survive in high-oxygen environments), and stimulates new blood vessel formation in damaged tissue.

### Inflammation and autoimmune conditions

Emerging research suggests HBOT can modulate the immune system, reducing chronic inflammation while preserving healthy immune function. Studies have shown benefits for traumatic brain injury (TBI), post-concussion syndrome, fibromyalgia, and certain autoimmune conditions, though much of this research is still in early stages.

### Athletic performance and recovery

Elite athletes increasingly use HBOT for accelerated recovery from injuries and intense training. The increased oxygen availability speeds muscle repair, reduces inflammation, and may enhance performance adaptation. Several professional sports teams in Europe and the US now have in-house hyperbaric chambers.

## What a Session Looks Like

### Before your session

- Wear comfortable, loose-fitting cotton clothing. Synthetic fabrics and anything that could generate static are typically not allowed inside the chamber.
- Remove all jewelry, watches, electronic devices, and metal objects.
- Avoid carbonated drinks before your session (the pressure changes can cause discomfort with gas in the stomach).
- If you have a cold or sinus congestion, inform the staff. Pressure changes can be uncomfortable or even painful when your sinuses are blocked.

### During the session

1. **Entering the chamber** — Depending on the facility, you will enter either a monoplace chamber (single-person, usually a clear acrylic tube where you lie down) or a multiplace chamber (a larger room that accommodates several people sitting upright). Aviv Clinics in Dubai uses multiplace chambers where you sit comfortably and breathe through an oxygen mask.

2. **Pressurization (5-10 minutes)** — The chamber gradually pressurizes to the target pressure (typically 1.5-2.0 ATA). During this phase, you will feel pressure building in your ears, similar to descending in an airplane. You will be instructed to equalize by swallowing, yawning, or performing the Valsalva maneuver (pinching your nose and gently blowing).

3. **Treatment phase (60-90 minutes)** — Once the target pressure is reached, you simply breathe normally through your mask or the ambient oxygen in the chamber. You can read, listen to music, meditate, watch content on a tablet (in multiplace chambers), or sleep. Many people find the experience deeply relaxing.

4. **Depressurization (5-10 minutes)** — The pressure is slowly returned to normal. You may feel a slight popping sensation in your ears.

5. **Post-session** — You can return to normal activities immediately. Some people feel energized after a session; others feel pleasantly fatigued and sleep deeply that night.

### Common side effects

HBOT is generally very safe when administered by trained professionals in accredited facilities. The most common side effects are:
- Temporary ear discomfort during pressurization (resolved by equalizing)
- Mild fatigue after sessions (especially during initial treatments)
- Temporary changes in vision (rare, usually resolves within weeks of completing a protocol)
- Light-headedness (uncommon, typically brief)

## Who Should Consider HBOT?

HBOT may be particularly beneficial if you:

- Are experiencing age-related cognitive decline or want to proactively protect brain health
- Have suffered a traumatic brain injury or concussion (even years ago)
- Are an athlete looking for advanced recovery and performance optimization
- Have chronic wounds or tissue damage that has not responded to conventional treatment
- Are interested in evidence-based age-reversal interventions
- Want to complement other longevity protocols (NAD+ therapy, exercise, nutrition)

### Who should avoid HBOT

- Individuals with untreated pneumothorax (collapsed lung)
- Those with certain types of ear conditions that prevent equalization
- People with claustrophobia (though multiplace chambers may be tolerable)
- Those taking certain medications (discuss with the clinic beforehand)
- Pregnant women (insufficient safety data)
